Hi, welcome to the Way of Conflict Management

A website that focuses on conflict resolution skills and methods.

Here, you can learn about a popular conflict style assessment tool - the Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI).

TKI was developed by Kenneth W. Thomas and Ralph H. Kilmann, and it can help you understand your tendencies and preferences when facing conflict.TKI divides conflict styles into five types: competing, compromising, avoiding, collaborating, and accommodating. These styles reflect your level of concern for your own needs and the needs of others in conflict situations.

Our website provides a brief introduction to these five styles, as well as some practical examples and suggestions. By reading our website, you can not only recognize your own conflict style, but also learn how to choose the most appropriate coping strategy according to different situations. This way, you can deal with and resolve conflicts more effectively, and improve your communication skills and interpersonal relationships.

Competing

This is a conflict style that focuses on one’s own needs and disregards the needs of others.

  • More

Collaborating

This is a conflict style that tries to find a solution that satisfies the concerns and interests of all parties.

  • More

Compromising

This is a conflict style that reaches a middle ground by giving up some of one’s own interests in exchange for satisfying some of the interests of others.

  • More

Avoiding

This is a conflict style that neither pursues one’s own needs nor addresses the concerns of others or resolves conflict by withdrawing.

  • More

Accommodating

This is a conflict style that prioritizes satisfying the needs of others over one’s own needs.
